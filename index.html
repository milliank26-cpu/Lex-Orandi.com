Introduction to Sacred Rubrics

The ordering of the Holy Sacrifice of the Mass is guided by centuries of sacred tradition and the strict norms of the universal Church. Every movement, gesture, and vestment carries a profound theological reality meant to direct the soul toward the heavenly liturgy.

This instruction platform serves as a modern companion to traditional customaries, ensuring clarity, consistency, and deep reverence across all liturgical minister cohorts within the parish community.

The celebration of the Eucharist is the action of Christ himself and of the Church. In it, Christ the Lord, through the ministry of the priest, offers himself, substantially present under the species of bread and wine, to God the Father and gives himself as spiritual food to the faithful who are associated with his offering.

General Instruction of the Roman Missal (GIRM), Paragraph 16

Sacristy and Altar Preparation Steps

Before the celebration of Mass begins, the assigned altar servers must execute the following ceremonial checklist systematically:

  1. Vest Compartment Check: Arrive at least twenty minutes prior to the scheduled Mass, vest in a clean, properly sized cassock and surplice, and observe sacred silence.
  2. Light the Altar Candles: Transfer flame reverently using the candle lighter, starting from the cross outwards. Light the Gospel side (left) first, followed by the Epistle side (right).
  3. Arrange the Sacred Vessels: Place the chalice, paten, purificator, pall, and corporal securely onto the credence table alongside the cruets of water and wine.
⚠️

Strict Liturgical Norm: Handling of Sacred Vessels

Only ordained ministers (Bishops, Priests, Deacons) and instituted acolytes are permitted to purify the sacred vessels following Holy Communion. Altar servers must handle unpurified chalices with utmost care and only carry them to and from the credence table as explicitly authorized.

Reverent Postures During the Liturgy

Maintaining clear, uniform postures throughout the sanctuary fosters visual beauty and interior focus. Compare the following specific instructions:

Reverent Gestures

Keep hands joined flat against the breast, palms touching, right thumb folded over the left in the form of a cross whenever moving without an object.

Prohibited Postures

Avoid letting hands hang loosely at the sides, crossing arms across the chest, or looking around into the congregation during the liturgy.
